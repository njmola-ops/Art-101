Paper 1

I was previously unfamiliar with autoethnography as a research method, but I thought it was a really interesting way to approach examining this issue. The format of this method allows for an in-depth examination of one person’s experience, which I feel provides more personal nuance than other qualitative methods. However, I think the autoethnography format creates more risk of researcher bias/influence on findings. This study seems pretty comprehensive, so I’m not sure if that applies here. Still, it’s something to consider. One thing this source does really well is provide background information on the issues being addressed. I feel like I learned a lot about racism denial and the specific contexts that enable it.

Paper 2

I found this source really interesting, especially as someone interested in the arts’ role in social advocacy. Photography is a really effective medium for capturing the candid experiences of both individuals and groups, so I can see why photovoice exhibitions might be compelling motivators for social change. My one critique is that this text did not provide a lot of evidence about how this program benefited the participants described in the study summary. I was curious about how this curriculum could have impacted the mental health communities involved. This source is more informative than descriptive/empirical and provides a lot of important details about how to successfully execute the program. For this reason, I’m not sure if my critique is completely necessary, as I found the text interesting and educational.

Paper 3

While I don’t have any direct critiques of this source, I would have appreciated more information on how new advances in technology will impact future archival practice and interpretation of materials. I think this is a really interesting direction to take in light of the information presented by this text and would like to understand how digitalization might effect past, present, and future data. I thought this source was very informational and liked that example transcripts were provided to give the reader a better understanding of the author’s point. One of this text’s strengths is that it provides a lot of background information on the history of both Milgram’s experiments and archival methods. I think accuracy of transcripts and archival interpretations are really important factors to consider when making broad generalizations about human behavior.
